Presto Mobile App Planner

By putting essential trip planning features into a complete package, our new PRESTO app is designed to make the transit planning process more efficient and easy to use.

My Role

UX researcher/designer. With the team of three other designers I worked collaboratively on each stage of the process that includes empathize, user research, definition and ideation, prototyping and testing stages.

Research

We started our user research by condicting a survey using Google Forms. The goal was to study out how often users take public transport and what tools they use to plan a commute.

HIGH IMPORTANCE QUESTIONS

  • When was the last time you took public transit?
  • How often do you take public transit?
  • Do you use any apps to plan your commute? If so, which?
  • Do you use multiple transit services for one trip? If so, which?
  • Are you satisfied with the level of service of your transit service?

User Persona

Based on user interviews, we defined our User Persona as a one for whom commuting to work is not optional. So the necessity to take public transport and be on time is highly important.

Definition & Ideation

With learning and studying the audience’s experience we compiled generalized User Insight, that was a base point in definig the Problem Statement.

Joseph wants a more efficient way of planning his commutes because, as of now, he finds himself using way too many apps to do so.

Feature Prioritization

We put forward our proposes for problem’s solution and emphasized four major features we could elaborate.

While “View parking availability at stations” and “Ability to share funds through app” seem effective, nonetheless it doesn’t solve current user’s need. Thence, we focused on adding scheduling and tracking functionalities to existing app.

User Journey

To evaluate at what particular moment our user feels frustrated we applied User Journey Map method. We found that searching information using multiple resources and switching between them is both confusing and annoying. The climactic bringdown happens when inconsistency in schedules and sometimes even absence of transport’s timetables cause missing the bas or train.

Problem Statement

During the user interviews, we discovered that there is no go-to app for transit planning. Therefore, we believe that accurate schedule planning is important and that we might be able to help if we can include that feature onto an existing app. We might do this by improving the Presto app functionality with reliable scheduling features. Doing this will allow our product to be the go-to app for GTA riders.

Competitor Analysis

To evaluate market we conducted Competitor Analysis. Our major contenders are Google Maps and Rocketman Mobile App. With certain advantages that these services have we found number of issues, among them ransit times might not be the most accurate at times, GPS detection can be iffy at times, won't update real-time location during travel.

Testing & Iteartions

We conducted 5 usability tests, compiled the feedback and taking into account all the usability testing’s findings completed iteration and transfered our prototypy into hi-fidelity version.

USABILITY TESTING NOTES

  • “Register” button was not clear
  • Navigation bar was unclear with all the same icons
  • “Planner” section was a little hard to locate
  • Unclear if certain elements on options page were clickable
  • Unclear on what “Track” button would do
  • Unclear about bus times page
  • Filler text was a bit off-putting
  • Certain filler elements caused more confusion
