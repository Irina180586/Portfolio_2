before we start what is presto? PRESTO is an electronic payment system that eliminates the need for tickets, tokens, passes and cash. PRESTO works across local transit in the Greater Toronto and Hamilton Area (GTHA) and Ottawa, making paying for your trip simple, convenient and secure.
overview the Problem Transit commuters are often frustrated when planning and taking their trips because they use multiple apps simultaneously.
we offer a solution By putting essential trip planning features into a complete package, our new PRESTO app is designed to make the transit planning process more efficient and easy to use.
allocation my role UX researcher/designer. With three other designers, I worked collaboratively on each stage of the process that includes empathizing, user research, definition and ideation, prototyping and testing stages.
research the approach We started our user research by condicting a survey using Google Forms. The goal was to study out how often users take public transport and what tools they use to plan a commute.
HIGH IMPORTANCE QUESTIONS
  • When was the last time you took public transit?
  • How often do you take public transit?
  • Do you use any apps to plan your commute? If so, which?
  • Do you use multiple transit services for one trip? If so, which?
  • Are you satisfied with the level of service of your transit service?

User Interviews With data received from survey we’ve dived deeper into understanding the audience's experience and conducted user interviews. The main obkectives of this stage were:
  • To understand past and present experiences with transit commuting
  • To understand the kind of services interviewees currently use for a commute (ie. bus tracking apps)
  • To understand the sort of issues or problems user face during the commute
insights We conducted 5 user interviews and all responds and notes were synthetized using Insight Map and Affinity Diagram methods.
user persona Based on user interviews, we defined our User Persona as a one for whom commuting to work is not optional. So the necessity to take public transport and be on time is highly important.

user persona To evaluate at what particular moment our user feels frustrated we applied User Journey Map method. We found that searching information using multiple resources and switching between them is both confusing and annoying. The climactic bringdown happens when inconsistency in schedules and sometimes even absence of transport’s timetables cause missing the bas or train.
Problem Statement During the user interviews, we discovered that there is no go-to app for transit planning. Therefore, we believe that accurate schedule planning is important and that we might be able to help if we can include that feature onto an existing app. We might do this by improving the Presto app functionality with reliable scheduling features. Doing this will allow our product to be the go-to app for GTA riders.
competitor analysis To evaluate market we conducted Competitor Analysis. Our major contenders are Google Maps and Rocketman Mobile App. With certain advantages that these services have we found number of issues, among them ransit times might not be the most accurate at times, GPS detection can be iffy at times, won't update real-time location during travel.
