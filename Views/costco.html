Costco Homepage Redesigning Project

Project Overview

Costco Wholesale is an American multinational corporation which operates a chain of membership-only big-box retail stores. Costco has a total of 103 locations and serves more than 10 million members across Canada. Because of the pandemic, many customers are switching from in-store shopping to online. However, we believe that Costco is missing an opportunity to convert these customers due to a user experience which overwhelms first-time visitors especially those who are used to targeted-shopping rather than browsing. That’s why we have chosen to redesign the Costco wholesale website.

Problem

Due to the pandemic, more people stopped shopping at actual stores and started to shop online. However, the cluttered Costco website doesn’t provide users quick and easy access to all products and deals, and it prevents them from shopping at Costco.

Solution

Redesign the existing website by organizing navigation systems and implementing intuitive UI design.

Research

We started our research phase by conducting a heuristic evaluation on the Costco.ca website to understand more about it and build problem assumptions, which were used for creating the user research plan later.

KEY FINDINGS

  • The homepage lacks white space, and the information on the page is overloaded. This gives people a cluttered and overwhelming impression.
  • It’s difficult to identify different content on the page since each section lacks headings.
  • The homepage is not easy to scan. Information needs to be categorized properly in order to create an intuitive user journey.
  • Location on the site is not easily identifiable because the navigation status doesn’t change and the existent of breadcrumbs is inconsistent.
  • Contact information is not very accessible for users. Several steps are needed to find the phone numbers of Costco customer service.

User Interviews

We also interviewed 6 users who have experienced shopping at Costco before. The interviews were conducted by asking a few open-ended questions related to defined objectives.

RESEARCH GOAL

Understand the behavior and insights of people who shop at Costco and their experience navigating through the Costco website.

OBJECTIVES

  • To learn more about the overall shopping experience at Costco (in-person or online)
  • To learn about customers’ needs and shopping experience
  • To learn the pain points in navigating the Costco homepage

User Interview Key Findings
  • Since the pandemic started, shoppers reduced their frequency of going in-stores, and they have never used Costco.ca for online shopping. This means regular customers simply stopped shopping with Costco.
  • Instead of shopping at Costco, people are going to less busy stores or buying groceries using other shopping websites such as Amazon and Walmart.
  • Users are not happy to shop at the Costco website because of its cluttered content and the overload of information makes customers want to leave the site.
  • People want to easily find deal information on the Costco website. The thrifty prices are the most popular reason why people shop at Costco.
  • Users do not like spending much time online shopping. Their behavior is more targeted-buying than browsing.
User Persona

Based on our user research data from the survey and user interviews, we created our user persona. This persona had been referenced often during our project when we make design decisions: "What does Sandra think about this?"

User Insight

As a full-time professional, Sandra wants to have better interaction by having faster and easier access to all products and deals on the website, as she wants to save time and money.

POV Template
Problem Statement

From our user research, we discovered that busy professionals who shop and provide essentials for their family do not always want to spend too much time browsing e-commerce websites. However, the cluttered Costco website doesn’t provide users an easy access to all products and deals and it prevents them from shopping. How can we transform the browsing user journey at Costco.ca into a valuable shopping experience by alleviating the mental stress from the overwhelming website so they can minimize time and money spent online?

Feature Prioritization Matrix

We used the feature prioritization matrix to determine which features we would like to focus on this project based on impact for users and feasibility.

DETERMINED FEATURES

  • Make the deals category easier to find
  • Organize content on the homepage by limiting the amount of information shown.
  • Make each section on the homepage distinguishable by adding proper headings and using different styles
  • Redesign the main navigation bar to provide an easier navigation system
User Journey Map

Information Architecture

Usability testings of the current website and user interviews confirmed that the navigation system and categorization of Costco.ca don’t fulfill its functions. Users can’t find items and sections of the company using an intuitive approach. Hence, we rearranged the website’s categorization, removed duplicated tabs, grouped sections that relate to the same section. Additionally, we changed some of the navigation tabs in order for users to perceive what categories are under the particular group.

Sitemap

Task Flows

These are the 4 tasks flows we did for our user testing.

The first task we ask user where can they find deals for laundry products.

Second task we ask users to look for in-store photo centre service.

Third task we ask users where they can find different categories of products.

Last task we ask users where they can find customer services help.

Sketches

After synthesizing the research data, we proceeded to design. Each member of our team presented a sketched ideas of a homepage layout. After a round of discussion, we’ve extracted elements that best aligned with our research takeaways and shaped the design concept for the Lo-Fi prototype.

Lo-Fi Prototype

The lo-Fi prototype was created based on our design concept. Other than the homepage we designed additional pages in order to have complete task flows for further usability testings.

Lo-Fi Prototype Usability Testing

The first round of Usability testing showed that navigation tabs naming doesn’t seem intuitive for users. E.g. users would never expect to look for Photo Services under the ‘’Warehouse Services’’ tab.

Another major finding was that putting the add-on link next to the section heading doesn’t appear as a help link to users. All testers didn’t notice that link.

Additionally to navigation and wording, backlogs in layout and design decisions were detected.

Based on all the insights from the first usability testings we iterated the prototype and move to the Mid-Fi version.

Mid-Fi Prototype

In the Mid-Fi version, we changed the titles of navigation tabs. We removed links which functioning failed at the first usability testings. We added dropdowns to the top and the main navigation reconsidered the categorization of the footer. Also, the prototype was enhanced with UI elements and brand colors.

Mid-Fi Usability Tests's Takeaways

The main purpose of this round of testing was to prove the changes we made in homepage sectioning.

We wanted to have the “Deals” section as a focal point of our users and it was important for us to understand the tester’s behavior and find out whether our design works as intended.

Usability testings proved our design concept and users successfully completed the task flow on finding the deals on a particular product using the main section of the homepage. However, having the option to filter the deals’ category felt overwhelming for testers and we decided to add this option to the assigned Deals page.
